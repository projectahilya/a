Our Mission

Why Ahilya Exists

"Why do girls in government schools across UP have no real safety infrastructure? Not an app. Not a helpline number on a poster. Actual infrastructure."

Most safety solutions are built by people who have never sat in a government school in Tier 2 UP and talked to a 13-year-old girl about what actually scares her. We have. That's where Ahilya started — and it drives every decision we make.

Our Story

About Project Ahilya

Founded in 2023. Built from scratch in a government school classroom in Varanasi. Still going.

Who We Are

Ground-up. Passionate. Real.

We are educators, activists, and young entrepreneurs who believe every girl has the right to be safe and to know how to protect herself. Ahilya started with a simple question — why do girls in government schools across UP have no real safety infrastructure?

Our programs use storytelling, interactive workshops, and community building to help girls learn about their bodies, their rights, and how to identify and report abuse. We teach boys too. Because safety is everyone's responsibility.

Founder: Praann Srivastava — entrepreneur, BHU B.Com graduate, pursuing MScFE at WorldQuant University. Built Ahilya from zero alongside multiple other ventures across events, marketing, and tech.

Know Your Rights

Legal Rights for
Women & Girls

Most girls don't know what laws protect them. Knowing your rights is the first step to protecting them.

Understanding the Law

Here's what the Indian Penal Code says about the most common forms of harassment and abuse — explained clearly, without legal jargon.

⚖ Updated to Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita (BNS) 2023 — effective July 1, 2024. The IPC has been replaced. These are the current laws.

BNS Section 74
Assault on Woman's Modesty

Any act of physical force or threat intended to cause fear, alarm, or outrage to a woman's sense of decency is a criminal offense. Includes unwanted touching, gestures, or any conduct that outrages a woman's modesty. Replaces IPC 354.

⚖ 1 to 5 years imprisonment + fine
BNS Section 75
Sexual Harassment

Unwelcome physical contact, demand for sexual favors, showing pornography without consent, or making sexually colored remarks all constitute sexual harassment under BNS. Covers workplaces and public spaces. Replaces IPC 354A.

⚖ Rigorous imprisonment up to 3 years + fine
BNS Section 77
Stalking

Following a woman, contacting her persistently despite clear disinterest, or monitoring her internet and electronic communication causing fear or distress is criminal stalking under BNS. Replaces IPC 354D.

⚖ First: up to 3 years | Repeat offence: up to 5 years + fine
BNS Section 79
Insulting Modesty / Catcalling

Using words, gestures, or acts intended to insult a woman's modesty — including catcalling, sexually suggestive remarks, or any utterance that intrudes on a woman's privacy in public — is a punishable offense. Replaces IPC 509.

⚖ Simple imprisonment up to 3 years + fine
BNS Section 76
Voyeurism

Watching or capturing images of a woman in a private act without consent, or disseminating such images, is criminal. Covers hidden cameras, recording without consent, and sharing intimate images. Replaces IPC 354C.

⚖ First: 1–3 years | Repeat: 3–7 years + fine
BNS Section 70
Rape & Sexual Assault

Sexual intercourse without consent is rape under BNS. Covers all forms of penetrative sexual assault. Marital rape of minors under 18 is explicitly criminalised. Replaces IPC 375/376.

⚖ Minimum 10 years rigorous imprisonment, up to life + fine
POCSO Act 2012
Protection of Children

The POCSO Act specifically protects children under 18 from sexual abuse, harassment, and exploitation. POCSO remains in full force alongside BNS and provides stricter protections for minors with stronger punishments.

⚖ Minimum 3 years to life imprisonment depending on offence
POSH Act 2013
Workplace Protection

Every organization with 10+ employees must have an Internal Complaints Committee. Every woman has the right to file a complaint, get a fair inquiry, and be protected from retaliation. Still fully in force.

⚖ Fine up to ₹50,000 for first offence | License cancellation for repeat
